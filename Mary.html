MARY T. BARRA
When asked “if there’s one thing men can do to improve women’s life at work”, Barra replied “stop making assumptions.”

Born in Michigan, Mary T. Barra first began working at GM as a university co-op student. Using her quiet determination
and ability to bring order to chaos, she steadily rose to her position in power, being made Chairman and CEO of General
Motors Company in 2016 and 2014, respectively; thus, making her the first woman to run one of the “big three” automakers
(Ford, Fiat Chrysler, GM) in the U.S.. She is the highest ranked female in the Fortune 500 at number 10.
