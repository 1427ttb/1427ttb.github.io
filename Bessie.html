BESSIE COLEMAN
“I decided blacks should not have to experience the difficulties I had faced, so I decided to open a flying school and teach other black women to fly.”

Bessie Coleman turned down by U.S. flight schools due to her colored skin, hence, she went to France in 1921 to earn the first
International Pilot’s License issued to an African American woman. She was called “Queen Bess” by aviation enthusiasts, flew at
exhibitions and wanted more colored people of both sexes to fly. She tried to open a flight school but passed away in an airshow.
