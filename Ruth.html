RUTH BADER GINSBURG
“Women will only have true equality when men share with them the responsibility of bringing up the next generation.”

Ruth Bader Ginsburg, born in New York, graduated first in her class at Columbia Law school. Post-graduation, she
faced difficulty when searching for a job due to discrimination and went to Sweden to join the Columbia Project on
International Civil Procedures, her other passion. When she returned from Sweden, she became a professor at Rutgers University
Law School prior to transferring to Columbia University. She led the fight against gender discrimination with the Women’s Rights
Project of the American Civil Liberties, arguing 6 landmark cases before the Supreme Court. She served on the U.S. Court of
Appeals per Jimmy Carter’s request prior to being appointed to Supreme Court by Bill Clinton in 1993. There, she continued to
fight for women’s rights while serving as justice. She is still is one of the most avid questioners on the bench today.
