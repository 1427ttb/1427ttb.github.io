JEANNETTE RANKIN
“I may be the first woman member of Congress,” she observed upon her election in 1916. “But I won’t be the last.”

Born in Montana, Jeannette Rankin was the first woman to hold office in Congress. Her political career began when
she joined a woman’s suffrage group at the University of Washington; from there, she became a professional lobbyist for the
National American Woman Suffrage Association. Her efforts in speaking at and organizing movements helped Montana women
gain the right to vote in 1914. She was later elected to Congress; during her career, she was the only Congress member to
vote against participation in both world wars. Her fearlessness in speaking up and taking controversial positions on several
burning issues were key factors in her leadership for women’s suffragist movement and her lifelong work as a pacifist.
