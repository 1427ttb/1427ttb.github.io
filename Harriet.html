HARRIET QUIMBY
“There is no reason why the aeroplane should not open up a fruitful occupation for women.”

Harriet Quimby became a licensed pilot in the U.S. in 1911 after being inspired by a story she was reporting She made great feats
by flying a moonlit night flight over Staten Island, New York and other exhibition flights. She became the first woman to fly solo
across the English Channel. Her achievements make her an inspiration to all women who feel that they cannot become pilots.
